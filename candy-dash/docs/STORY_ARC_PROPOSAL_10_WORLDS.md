## 0. The canon wrinkle, solved in-fiction

Episode 7.5 says **5 corrupted pools**. Jonathan wants **10 worlds**. Fix that
preserves 7.5 word-for-word:

> The five red pools were only the corruption Angus could *see*. About 20
> Saurosapiens fell; as Troll cleanses each realm, the surviving interlopers and
> the corruption itself **flee deeper along the Wood's root-ways** — the living
> network that connects every portal tree. Cerebra (whose glyphs numbered the
> original five) detects the bleed after realm 3: "The corruption is not
> retreating. It is *migrating*." Five surface realms become five **Deep Realms**
> beneath/beyond them. The mission doubles; nobody is happy; Sparkles is thrilled. (Jonathan editor: lmao!)

This also supplies the darkness ramp for free: surface realms (1–5) are corrupted
*by accident* (the Spillover); the deep realms (6–10) are corrupted *on purpose* —
something is feeding it.

## 1. The spine in one paragraph

Troll and Sparkles cleanse ten increasingly wrong realms to reopen the Wood's
portals and earn Troll's body back. Along the way the corruption stops looking
like an accident: it carries a **machine signal** (Paleotech protocol), it
*learns*, and the redeemed Saurosapien bosses keep repeating the same broken
promise — *"We were told we would get our world back."* The final purification
reassembles the Stabilizing Matrix, unlocks every pool, and gets Troll home a
hero — just as the prehistoric-Earth pool flares on its own and shows them
future Earth, Susie Model 002 walking into Dr D's volcano facility, and a
time-travel signature Cerebra recognizes as **Eldaryn**. The attack on Angus's
realm was never random. Cut to black; continue in Episode 8.

## 3. The King Angus World-2 opening beat (as requested)

Cold open of World 2, before level 2-1. Angus's face swirls into view in a vortex:

> **KING ANGUS:** "Hold it right there laddie! I've been watchin' from the big
> pool — ye're shootin' those poor corrupted critters TOO HARD! They're no'
> villains, they're *patients*! Gentle-like, aye? Let the magic do the work."
>
> **TROLL:** "Ye want me to shoot them… *softly*."
>
> **ANGUS:** "Aye! Softly! With FEELING! Hohoho!" *(vortex pops)*
>
> **SPARKLES:** "I alwayth thoot with feeling, Thpooth."

**Mechanic change from 2-1 onward (and retroactively everywhere):** purified
critters no longer hop off-screen and despawn. They stay in the level, wandering
cutely and harmlessly (no collision damage, idle grazing/fluttering behaviors).
The level visibly *heals* as you play — by the exit, a cleansed level is full of
life. This is cheap to build (keep the existing purified sprites, swap the
hop-away despawn for a wander behavior) and it becomes the game's signature image.

Optional payoff escalation (see Idea 1 below): critters gather near the exit
tree/portal to see you off; late worlds, they help.

## 4. Five plot-development ideas for the game

Each is a thread woven across all ten worlds, not a one-off.

### Idea 1 — The Critters Remember
The persistent purified critters (Angus's beat) grow into the emotional engine.
World 2–3: they wander. World 4–5: they follow Troll at a shy distance and
gather at level exits. World 6–8: they start *helping* — a Soarer lifts Troll to
a secret ledge, a Stonetooth shoulders open a blocked path (soft-gating optional
areas by kindness: your Goodness score literally opens routes). World 10: every
species you ever healed pours into the Convergence to shield Troll during the
final purification. Troll, who started the game complaining about escort duty,
ends it escorted. Angus's whole lesson lands without a single speech.

### Idea 2 — The Signal in the Corruption
The mystery layer. Optional Lore Stones (already canon, already built into the
artifact system) each decode one fragment: the corruption pulses in a machine
rhythm → the rhythm matches a Paleotech homing protocol → the protocol is
running on *Eldaryn* hardware → the hardware doesn't exist yet. Players who
collect everything can call the ending's twist one world early — perfect
replay/completionist bait, and it stitches the game to episodes 6–7 (the same
homing signal that took Captain T-Rex and Model 002).

### Idea 3 — Two Suzies, Seen From Far Away
The game never resolves the Suzies — it *aches* toward them. Vision pools
(worlds 4 and 8) and hub cutscenes let Timmy glimpse "Susie" in two places at
once; Cerebra runs the math and won't share the result; Sparkles asks the
question out loud in the Fen and nobody answers. Hub scenes between worlds
track Timmy's arc from cheerleader to quietly determined little brother — by
world 9 he's not asking Troll to win, he's asking Troll to *hurry*. Sets the
emotional table for the comic's Model 001/002 storyline without stepping on it.

### Idea 4 — Vos's Shame, Told Sideways
The memory-pool fragments (worlds 5 and 9) tell the blackmail backstory:
a captured Eldaryn away-team officer, dosed and addicted by Saurosapien captors,
forced to open Eldaryn defenses. The game never says "Vos betrayed his people" —
it shows a young officer in trouble and lets the comic pay it off. Meanwhile
every boss redemption echoes it: each Saurosapien was also coerced, promised,
used. By world 10 the player understands the Saurosapiens are the *second*
victims of whoever is really steering — which is exactly the thematic ground
("power without wisdom becomes corruption") the GDD locked.

### Idea 5 — The Corruption That Learns
The escalation layer. Surface realms: corruption as spilled poison (static,
environmental). Deep realms: corruption as *student* — it copies mechanics
(Mirror boss), then copies Troll (Mirrorglass Fen), then copies *home* (Withered
Wood), then tries to become a new thing entirely (Convergence). The dread ramps
because the threat gets more personal each world, not just harder. And it gives
the finale its stakes: this is the last moment the corruption can ever be
purified — after the Convergence it would be *born*, and cleansing would become
killing. Troll's one chance to win the kind way. He takes it.

## 5. The ending and the cliffhanger (bridge to Episode 8)

1. **Victory.** The Matrix reassembles; ten realms bloom gold in one montage;
   Troll and Sparkles surf the reconnected root-ways home.
2. **Homecoming.** The Wood restored, pools unlocked. Angus keeps his word —
   begins the rite to restore Troll's true form. Timmy hugs Troll's leg.
   Cerebra smiles. It is, for eight seconds, a perfect ending.
3. **The turn.** The prehistoric-Earth pool — the one Cerebra has been waiting
   on all game — flares *on its own*. No one opened it. Through it: not
   prehistoric Earth but **future Earth** — the volcano facility, and Susie
   Model 002 walking zombie-straight down an evac ramp into Paleotech's hangars
   (continuous with ep 7 scene 1).
4. **The signature.** Above the vision, Cerebra reads the energy braid:
   *Eldaryn temporal displacement.* Someone with her people's technology is
   moving through time. The Captain's words land: "…the voice of your friend."
5. **Smash cut.** Angus, quiet, no "hohoho": *"The corruption wasnae an
   accident, laddie. Somebody built it a door… and we just opened all of ours."*
   Troll's body-restoration rite is left mid-cast, unfinished. **TO BE
   CONTINUED IN EPISODE 8.**

What this buys the comic: Episode 8 can pick up with the full cast in the Wood,
portals open, Troll mid-restoration (a reason he's still in troll form as long
as the story wants), the Chani/Vos/time-travel plot armed, Model 002 delivered
to Dr D, Model 001 with Bob underground, and a party with a reason to split:
someone must go to future Earth (Suzies/Vos/Paleotech) and someone must hold
the Wood. The "how will X get Y back" engine: **Timmy has to get his sister
back — and there are two of her.**

  "Season 1" of the game (matches ep 7.5 exactly); the Midpoint twist in §2 is
  written to be the season break. Deep Realms 6–10 become the game's own
  sequel/expansion — same file, second campaign.
- The §3 critter-persistence change is small and should land in the next build
  pass regardless of everything else — biggest heart-per-hour ratio in this doc.
- Boss art: the real Saurosapien look is still TBD (bible §5 note). Worlds 2–8
  bosses can use the critter-pair strategy; the Captain (worlds 9–10) is the one
  that genuinely needs the armored-reptilian design before he can appear.
